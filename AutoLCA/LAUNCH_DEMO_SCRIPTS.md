# Triya.io v1.0 Launch Demo Scripts
*Target Audience: EHS Managers, R&D Engineers, CFOs (Carbon Liability)*

## Example 1: Aerospace Titanium Bracket (AM vs CNC)
**Goal:** Show how Additive Manufacturing (AM) reduces material waste vs subtractive.

- **Phase 1: GOAL.** 
  - *Click:* Goal & Scope Panel.
  - *Script:* "We start with a critical Aerospace bracket. Our functional unit is 1 flight-certified part. Note the System Boundary: we're including Module D recycling credits to see the true circular value of titanium scrap."
- **Phase 2: INVENTORY.**
  - *Click:* Inventory Stage. Drag/Pan the 10ndoe graph.
  - *Script:* "Observe the deep technosphere. We're tracking the Kroll process energy intensity and the specific high-purity Argon gas required for atomization. Each node here is pulling real-time data from our local industrial database."
- **Phase 3: IMPACT.**
  - *Click:* 'Calculate' -> Multi-Impact Tab.
  - *Script:* "Calculating results... Notice that while AM uses more electricity, it slashes Material Scarcity. Look at the Pivot Table: our GWP per kg of finished part is 40% lower than traditional CNC because we aren't milling away 90% of the block."
- **Phase 4: INTERPRETATION.**
  - *Click:* 'Interpretation' -> Monte Carlo.
  - *Script:* "Is the data reliable? Our Monte Carlo simulation shows a tight bell curve for Climate Change. Let's run the AI Consultant... it highlights that switching to a green-hydrogen sponge source would reduce our carbon liability by a further 12%."

## Example 2: NMC 811 EV Battery Cell 
**Goal:** Highlighting Toxicity and Supply Chain Risk.

- **Phase 1: GOAL.**
  - *Script:* "Here we model the next-gen NMC 811 cell. Our cutoff criteria is set at 0.1% to capture trace minerals like Cobalt and Nickel, which are our highest environmental risk factors."
- **Phase 2: INVENTORY.**
  - *Script:* "We have 15 interconnected nodes. Note the China-centric processing locations. We're modelling the massive energy draw of the Cleanroom HVAC system—often an invisible hotspot in battery LCA."
- **Phase 3: IMPACT.**
  - *Script:* "The Impact Panel shows a massive hit on Human Toxicity. By drilling down into the contribution analysis, we see that the NMP solvent evaporation is the primary driver."
- **Phase 4: INTERPRETATION.**
  - *Script:* "Exporting the final JRC-compliant report... the Sensitivity Tornado chart shows that the Battery's total footprint is 70% dependent on the carbon-intensity of the Chinese grid at the cathode coating stage."

  - *Script:* "Focus on the Agricultural nodes. We're tracking wet-milling water demand and phosphate-based eutrophication in the US Iowa cluster."

- **Phase 4: INTERPRETATION.**
  - *Script:* "The AI Auditor confirms our trade-off analysis is ISO 14044 compliant. Final export to PDF captures this land-use tradeoff for the sustainability report."

## Example 4: Industrial Wind Turbine Blade (100m)
**Goal:** Large-scale Infrastructure and Capital Goods.

- **Phase 1: GOAL.**
  - *Script:* "Modeling a 100-meter offshore blade. The goal is to determine the 'Carbon Payback Time' over a 50-year operational life."
- **Phase 2: INVENTORY.**
  - *Script:* "Massive flows here. We're looking at 20 tons of Glass Fiber and 5 tons of Epoxy Resin. Note the complexity of the Vacuum Infusion process node."
- **Phase 3: IMPACT.**
  - *Script:* "The Impact Assessment shows the huge 'embodied' footprint of the resins. However, when we normalize against the 50-year energy output, the footprint per MWh is negligible."
- **Phase 4: INTERPRETATION.**
  - *Script:* "Running the Sensitivity analysis on End-of-Life: Pyrolysis for fiberglass recovery vs Landfill. The interpretation engine proves that pyrolysis saves 5 tons of CO2e per blade."
